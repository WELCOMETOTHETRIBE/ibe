04

What backs a verdict

Deterministic & formally checked

The accept/refuse decision is a pure function of structured inputs — no model, no prompt, no discretion. The capability and promotion lifecycles are specified in TLA+ and checked in CI by an explicit-state model checker that also proves deliberately-unsafe transitions are caught.

Capability lifecycle28 states · SAFE
Capability — fault injected44 states · CAUGHT
Promotion lifecycle34 states · SAFE
Promotion — fault injected46 states · CAUGHT
Proven properties
  • A revoked or expired capability cannot be used.
  • A single-use capability cannot be used twice.
  • A builder cannot issue its own capability.
  • Production promotion cannot occur before verification.
  • A refused change cannot be promoted.
  • The builder cannot mark itself verified.
  • A failed deployment reaches rollback or a safe state.

Stated plainly

  • Execution isolation: the Docker runner is real, but where Docker is absent the honestly-labeled local runner runs and certificates record isolated = false. IBE never claims isolation it did not get.
  • Policy: the deterministic TypeScript engine is authoritative; Rego/OPA is a spec mirror.
  • Formal: TLA+ specs are authoritative; the TS checker is the CI gate (TLC not vendored).
  • Provenance / compliance: in-toto/SLSA-structured and an OSCAL subset — not SLSA-certified, not CMMC.
